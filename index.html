The Toffifee Story

1903
August Storck opens a small candy factory in Werther, Germany — the company that would one day create Toffifee.
1973
Toffifee is born in West Germany! The world's first candy combining a caramel cup, whole hazelnut, nougat cream, and chocolate cap. Originally marketed as an energy snack for working women.
Late 1970s
Early TV ads in Germany position Toffifee as a fun, family-oriented treat. The iconic brown box design becomes instantly recognizable.
1980s
The famous tagline arrives: "There's so much fun in Toffifee!" Toffifee expands beyond Germany to conquer European shelves.
1990s
Toffifee enters the US market — rebranded as "Toffifay" with a white box design. The rest of the world keeps the original brown box and spelling.
2016
Toffifee reaches the milestone of being sold in over 100 countries. From Berlin to Tokyo to Toronto — the world is hooked.
Today
Toffifee remains one of the world's most beloved boxed candies — a go-to gift, a nostalgic treat, and a perfect four-layer masterpiece.

Make Your Own Toffifee

Can't wait for the store? Here's how to make a homemade version that's surprisingly close to the real thing.

Homemade Toffifee

Caramel cups with hazelnut, nougat & chocolate

⏱
20 min
Prep
🔥
15 min
Cook
❄
1 hour
Chill
🍴
~20 pcs
Yield

Ingredients

Caramel Cups
Granulated sugar200 g
Glucose / corn syrup2 tbsp
Heavy cream150 ml
Butter25 g
Water50 ml
Pinch of salt—
Filling
Whole hazelnuts, roasted~20
Nutella or nougat spread100 g
Topping
Dark chocolate100 g

Method

1
Toast the hazelnuts. Spread hazelnuts on a baking tray and roast at 180°C (350°F) for 10–12 minutes until golden and fragrant. Rub off any loose skins with a clean towel. Set aside.
2
Make the caramel. Combine sugar, water, and glucose syrup in a saucepan. Heat over medium, stirring until sugar dissolves. Stop stirring once it boils. Let it cook until it reaches a golden amber color (about 125°C / 257°F). Remove from heat and carefully stir in the cream and butter — it will bubble up. Stir in salt. Let it cool for 2–3 minutes.
3
Shape the cups. Pour caramel into a lightly oiled silicone mini muffin mold, filling each about halfway. Let it cool for 5 minutes, then use oiled fingers (or the back of a small spoon) to press a hollow into each, pushing caramel up the sides to form a cup shape. Chill for 15 minutes.
4
Fill them up. Press one roasted hazelnut into the center of each caramel cup. Spoon or pipe nougat/Nutella around the hazelnut, filling the cup to just below the rim.
5
Add the chocolate cap. Melt dark chocolate over a double boiler (or in 20-second microwave bursts, stirring between). Spoon a small disc of melted chocolate on top of each filled cup, smoothing it to cover the nougat. Chill in the fridge for at least 30 minutes until set.
6
Unmold and enjoy! Gently pop the Toffifee out of the mold. Store in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 2 weeks (if they last that long).
Pro tip: If you have an empty Toffifee tray, use it as a mold for an authentic shape! Otherwise, a silicone half-sphere or mini muffin mold works perfectly. For an extra touch, drizzle a thin line of white chocolate on top.
